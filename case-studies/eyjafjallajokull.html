🎯 Eyjafjallajökull, Iceland (2010)

A-Level Geography - Hazards ➜ Volcanic Eruption - MEDC

Date (peak)14 – 20 Apr 2010
VEI4
PlatesNorth American ↔ Eurasian (divergent) + Icelandic hotspot
Volcano typeStratovolcano under 200 m ice cap
Lat/Long63.633° N, −19.62° W
Exam-friendly tag line“Small Icelandic blast that shut the whole damn sky over Europe.”

🚀Causes

  • Magma source: Rising basaltic melt on the Mid-Atlantic Ridge is juiced by a local mantle plume ➜ steady magma supply.
  • Interaction with ice cap: Magma met a significant amount of meltwater, turning a gentle fissure eruption (Mar) into a phreatomagmatic ash bomb (Apr).
  • Tectonic setting: 5 cm yr⁻¹ plate separation cracks the crust; magma exploited a south-west fissure on the volcano’s flank.

💥Primary Impacts

  • 800 locals evacuated; zero deaths but significant disruption.
  • 700+ families’ farmland covered with ash & fluorine-rich grit; livestock penned indoors.
  • Meltwater bursts (jökulhlaups) damaged main Ring Road bridges and caused flooding in the Markarfljót valley.
  • Ash plume reached 9 km high, depositing 10 million m³ of tephra over south Iceland in 24 hours.
  • Airspace lockdown: 100,000 flights cancelled across Europe from April 15-20.

📉Secondary Impacts

  • Global airline industry lost ≈ US $1.7 billion; 10 million passengers stranded.
  • Kenyan flower exports rotted (lost ≈ US $3 million day⁻¹).
  • Icelandic tourism eventually boomed due to increased global awareness.
  • CO₂ drop: 2.8 million tonnes of emissions saved from grounded jets — a minor positive environmental impact.
  • Health: Minor respiratory issues in parts of Europe; greater concern for Icelandic farmers from silica-rich dust.

🧰Immediate Responses (0-3 months)

  • State-run ICE-SAR teams and Red Cross managed evacuations to community centres.
  • Sections of Route 1 were bulldozed open quickly after floodwater subsided to maintain south-coast transport.
  • Volcanic Ash Advisory Centre (VAAC) London issued six-hourly ash-cloud maps; Eurocontrol enforced a zero-tolerance flight ban.
  • Livestock given fluoride-binding feed to prevent dental and bone damage.

🔧Long-Term Responses / Effects

  • Icelandic Met Office installed more seismic & GPS equipment on ice-capped volcanoes for improved early-warning.
  • EU & ICAO revised ash-flight rules: planes can now fly in “low concentration” zones.
  • Rebuilt bridges/embankments on Route 1 with deeper culverts for future jökulhlaups.
  • Ash-enriched soils led to greener pastures and bumper hay yields for local farmers by 2012.
  • "Inspired by Iceland" tourism brand launched in 2010; visitor numbers doubled by 2016.

✂️“Bare-Minimum Facts” (for quick recall)

2010 Eyjafjallajökull (VEI-4) erupted beneath ice. Ash plume closed European airspace for five days (100k flights, US$1.7 bn loss). 800 evacuated; jökulhlaups wrecked Route 1. Caused by divergent plate margin + hotspot. Long term: new monitoring, revised ash-flight rules, and a significant Iceland tourism surge.
